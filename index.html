01 · Philosophy

A site that feels more like a journal than a product.

This redesign moves away from glossy template energy and toward something calmer: an authored space with a paper-like background, a serif-led voice, quiet motion, and a more reflective pace.

Design intention
“

Make the page feel held together by typography, rhythm, and silence.

The beauty of this direction comes from restraint. Fewer loud surfaces. Fewer decorative effects. More attention to spacing, texture, wording, and the emotional temperature of the page.

This first pass interprets your idea of “Japanese beauty” as editorial calm: warm neutrals, dark ink text, subtle moss green, and a gentle sense of pause.

What stays Personal writing, technology, life, a human voice.
What changes The site stops looking like a generic modern template and starts looking more authored.
What comes next A clearer archive, individual essay pages, and a more coherent long-term visual system.

About me

Dongyuan

I’m interested in technology not only as a technical system, but as something that shapes attention, relationships, work, and identity. This site is where technical notes and personal reflections can sit next to each other without apology.

The long-term goal is simple: build a site that feels calm enough to read, specific enough to matter, and durable enough to grow over time.
